# Superstore Sales & Profit Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the **Sample Superstore** dataset to uncover business insights related to sales performance, profitability, discounts, customer segments, shipping methods, and regional performance.

Using **Python** for data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), visualization, and statistical analysis, this project identifies factors affecting profitability and provides data-driven recommendations that can help improve business performance.

---

## Business Problem

Retail businesses often generate high sales without achieving high profits. This analysis aims to answer questions such as:

* Which products and categories generate the highest profit?
* Which products consistently lose money?
* How do discounts affect profitability?
* Which regions perform best and worst?
* Are there seasonal sales trends?
* Which shipping methods are used most frequently?
* What business decisions can increase profitability?

## Key Findings

### 1. Furniture has the weakest profitability

Although Furniture generates significant revenue, **Tables** and **Bookcases** consistently record losses, making them the least profitable sub-categories.

**Business Impact**

Revenue alone is not an indicator of success. Certain products generate sales while reducing overall profit.

---

### 2. Heavy discounts significantly reduce profit

Orders with discounts greater than **20%** frequently result in negative profits.

Correlation analysis also showed a **negative relationship between Discount and Profit**, indicating that aggressive discounting erodes margins.

**Business Impact**

Large discounts increase sales volume but substantially reduce profitability.

---

### 3. Technology is the most profitable category

Technology products produce the highest profit margins among all product categories.

**Business Impact**

Technology products should receive higher inventory priority and marketing investment.

---

### 4. Sales peak during Quarter 4

Sales increase significantly during the final quarter of the year.

**Business Impact**

The business should prepare inventory, staffing, and logistics before the holiday season to avoid stock shortages.

---

### 5. Central Region underperforms

Compared with the other regions, the Central region contributes less profit.

**Business Impact**

Pricing strategies, discount policies, and product mix in this region should be reviewed.

---

### 6. Standard Class is the dominant shipping method

Most customer orders are shipped using Standard Class.

**Business Impact**

Since most customers choose Standard Class, operational improvements in this shipping mode will have the greatest overall impact.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

* Review pricing strategy for Tables and Bookcases.
* Limit discounts above **20%** or require management approval for larger discounts.
* Prioritize Technology products in marketing campaigns and inventory planning.
* Increase inventory levels before Quarter 4 to meet seasonal demand.
* Investigate pricing and promotional strategies in the Central region.
* Evaluate whether premium shipping options encourage customer retention and repeat purchases.
